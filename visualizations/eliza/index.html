Sources and historical limits

ELIZA was developed by Joseph Weizenbaum at MIT during 1964–1966 in MAD-SLIP on CTSS. ELIZA was the interpreter; DOCTOR was one conversational script. The guided transcript and compact rule set here are fictional, representative adaptations for education, not a recovered conversation or exact emulator.

The recovered 1965 listing and attached DOCTOR materials are licensed by MIT Libraries under the included software license. This page does not reproduce the full script. Implementations and surviving historical versions differ.
