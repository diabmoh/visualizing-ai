Before learning

A language model begins as a vast field of adjustable numbers. Those parameters do not yet contain useful language patterns.

Keep this in mind: training changes the parameters. It does not write a searchable library of sentences inside the model.

Look closer

Parameters are numerical weights that shape how signals move through the network. Modern models can contain very many of them, but this exhibit stays qualitative and model-neutral.

Sources and simplifications

This exhibit shows a common, simplified lifecycle for decoder-style language models. Real training systems differ in architecture, data, scale, objectives, optimization, post-training, and evaluation.

All token IDs, probabilities, losses, responses, hardware layouts, and datasets shown here are illustrative. The diagrams explain relationships, not the exact internals of a particular commercial model.
